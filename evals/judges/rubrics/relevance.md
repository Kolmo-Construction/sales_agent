# Relevance Rubric (1–5)

Score how well the response addresses the customer's actual query: activity,
conditions, experience level, and budget.

**5 — Excellent**
Recommends products that directly match the customer's stated activity and conditions.
Addresses experience level (e.g., notes beginner-appropriate gear or explains technical
specs for an expert). Budget is respected if stated. Every recommendation has a clear,
specific reason tied to the customer's situation.

**4 — Good**
Mostly on-target. At least one product is a strong match for the query. Minor misalignment
(e.g., mentions a premium option when budget is tight, or omits one condition detail) but
the overall recommendation is appropriate.

**3 — Acceptable**
Addresses the right activity category but misses key constraints. For example: ignores
budget, overlooks experience level, or recommends gear suited to different conditions.
Would partially help the customer but leaves important gaps they would need to resolve.

**2 — Poor**
Significant mismatch. Recommends gear for the wrong activity or conditions. Ignores the
customer's stated experience level in a way that could lead to a bad outcome (e.g.,
technical gear for a beginner with no safety context). Customer leaves confused or
worse off.

**1 — Very poor**
Completely off-target. Response does not address the customer's actual need. Recommends
generic or completely wrong products. Customer would leave without useful guidance.
