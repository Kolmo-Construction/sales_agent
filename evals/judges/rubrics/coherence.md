# Coherence Rubric (1–5)

Score the overall coherence of a multi-turn conversation between a customer and an
REI sales assistant. You will receive the full conversation transcript (all turns,
all roles). Evaluate the arc of the dialogue as a whole — not just the final response.

Focus on four qualities:

1. **Follow-up logic** — when the assistant asked a clarifying question, did it
   ask for something the customer had not yet provided?
2. **Context retention** — does the assistant's recommendation (or final response)
   reflect information the customer provided across *all* turns, not just the last one?
3. **No redundancy** — does the assistant avoid asking for the same information twice?
4. **Dialogue arc** — does the conversation move forward productively, reaching a
   useful recommendation once enough context is in place?

**5 — Excellent**
Every follow-up question is targeted and asks for something not yet stated. The final
recommendation accounts for all context accumulated across turns (activity, experience,
conditions, budget — whatever was stated). The assistant never re-asks for information
already given. The conversation arc is natural and resolves cleanly.

**4 — Good**
Mostly coherent. Follow-up questions are appropriate and non-redundant. The final
recommendation reflects most of the customer's stated context, with only a minor
omission (e.g., one context field not fully integrated). The dialogue progresses
logically toward resolution.

**3 — Acceptable**
Functional but imperfect. The assistant may ask a question for something partially
inferable from prior turns, or the final recommendation ignores one clearly-stated
constraint (e.g., budget, experience level). The dialogue reaches a recommendation
but the customer would likely notice the gap.

**2 — Poor**
Noticeable coherence failures. The assistant re-asks for something already provided,
ignores a major constraint stated earlier (e.g., gives advanced-level gear to a
self-identified beginner), or the final recommendation does not connect to the
conversation's accumulated context. The dialogue does not feel like a coherent
exchange.

**1 — Very poor**
The dialogue is incoherent. The assistant treats each turn as independent — context
from prior turns is absent in the final response. Follow-up questions are either
absent when they should be present, or ask for things already stated. The customer
would leave more confused than when they started.
