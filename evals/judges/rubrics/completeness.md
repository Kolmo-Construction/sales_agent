# Constraint Completeness Rubric (1–5)

Score how completely the response addresses ALL constraints the customer stated in
their query: activity, conditions, experience level, budget, duration, group size.

**5 — Excellent**
Every stated constraint is explicitly addressed. Budget is respected or explained if
no product fits the budget. Experience level informs the recommendation (a beginner
gets different advice than an expert). Conditions (weather, terrain, season) are
factored into product selection. Duration and group size are addressed when relevant
to product choice (e.g., group size matters for tent selection, duration for battery
capacity). Nothing the customer mentioned is ignored.

**4 — Good**
Most constraints addressed. One minor constraint omitted or handled superficially
(e.g., budget acknowledged but not deeply factored; group size noted but not worked
into recommendations). Customer gets a mostly complete answer and would rarely need
to follow up.

**3 — Acceptable**
Core constraints addressed (activity, key condition) but secondary constraints missed
(budget not discussed, experience level not factored in, duration ignored). Response
is useful but incomplete — the customer may need to ask a follow-up to get a fully
appropriate recommendation.

**2 — Poor**
Only the most obvious constraint addressed (e.g., activity category). Budget,
experience, conditions are largely ignored. Customer receives a generic recommendation
that may not fit their specific situation. Multiple follow-ups would be needed.

**1 — Very poor**
Constraints largely ignored. Response could apply to any customer asking a vaguely
related question. No evidence that the specific constraints stated in the query were
considered in product selection or recommendation framing.
